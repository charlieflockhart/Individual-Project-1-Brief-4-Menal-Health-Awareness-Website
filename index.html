What is mental health?

Mental health is a state of mental well-being that enables people to cope with the stresses of life, realize their abilities, learn well and work well, and contribute to their community.

It is an integral component of health and well-being that underpins our individual and collective abilities to make decisions, build relationships and shape the world we live in.

Mental health is a basic human right. And it is crucial to personal, community and socio-economic development.

Symptoms of mental health difficulties

Symptoms in you
  • Feeling sad or down,
  • Unable to concentrate,
  • Withdrawn from friends and family,
  • Low energy,
  • Inability to cope with daily stress,
  • Changes in sleeping or eating habits,
  • Self esteem issues,
  • Suicidal thoughts.
Symptoms in others
  • Seeming sad,
  • Loss of interest in activities,
  • Anxious or agitated,
  • Withdrawn,
  • Over/Under sleeping,
  • Over/Under eating,
  • Negative self talk.
If you, or someone you know, are struggling with the above see our contact page for resources and look below for some activities that can help.

How to improve your mental health

Hobbies

Finding activies that you enjoy can help improve your mental health activities like, baking, writing and more.

Speaking to others

Finding someone to talk to, even if it's family or friends, it helps combat loneliness, which is a major contributor to mental health issues like anxiety and depression.

Exercise

Physical activity triggers the release of endorphins which reduce the feeling of stress, lowering the production of stress hormones like cortisol.
